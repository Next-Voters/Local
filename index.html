What's new in San Francisco?

Presented by NV

MAY 4, 2026  |  SAN FRANCISCO

IMMIGRATION

Mayor Proposes $195M From 2024 Bond

  • Mayor Lurie proposed spending up to $195 million from the voter-approved 2024 bond.
  • Funds would upgrade neighborhood health centers and public hospitals.
  • A portion is earmarked for street safety and public space improvements.
  • The proposal follows passage of the Healthy, Safe, and Vibrant SF bond.

$8.5M Lifeline Loan Considered for SF Zoo

  • The Budget and Finance Committee will weigh a loan of up to $8.5 million to the SF Zoological Society.
  • Repayment would span 10 years through management fee deductions.
  • The lease could be terminated if loan terms are not met.
  • A separate item would shift $2.5 million to fund the loan in FY 2025-26.

Committee Backs $416K to Replace Voting Systems

  • The Budget and Finance Committee voted 3-0 to recommend a $416,431 elections grant.
  • Funds replace voting and election management systems through August 2026.
  • The committee also backed a $14.64B FY 2025-26 Gann spending limit, 3-0.
  • Both items now move forward for full board consideration.

CIVIL RIGHTS

State Bill Would Fast-Track High-Rises Near Transit

  • AB 2074 would require California's seven biggest cities to create downtown transit hub districts by mid-2027.
  • High-density housing inside those districts would get streamlined ministerial approvals.
  • A new state Downtown Revitalization Loan Fund would run through CalHFA.
  • Eligibility ties to density, affordability, and labor standards.

SF Proposes Its Own Rules for Transit-Area Housing

  • The mayor introduced an Alternative Plan to state SB 79 for transit-area housing.
  • It would exclude industrial hubs and some distant sites.
  • The plan adds height and density allowances near transit.
  • It will be sent to state housing officials with required findings.

$30M Financing Plan for 1687 Market Housing

  • A resolution was introduced to authorize up to $30 million in revenue notes.
  • Funds support a 94-unit multifamily rental project at 1687 Market Residences.
  • The developer is Mercy Housing California 120, L.P.
  • The item heads to the Budget and Finance Committee for a hearing.

One Oak Podium Could Rise on Market Street

  • A zoning amendment would raise the allowed podium height at 1500-1540 Market Street.
  • Projects not using HC-SF could go from 120 feet to 140 feet on those parcels.
  • Supervisor Dorsey introduced the legislation.
  • The Land Use Committee will review next.

ECONOMY

150 Blocks Targeted for Repaving with SB 1 Funds

  • Mayor Lurie introduced a bill prioritizing 150 blocks for street repaving.
  • Work would draw on roughly $33 million from the state SB 1 program.
  • Targeted blocks span multiple neighborhoods.
  • The bill covers maintenance and broader road upgrades.

SF Releases Updated Climate Action Plan

  • The mayor released a five-year update to the city's Climate Action Plan.
  • A signed law updates the city's climate goals to match the new plan.
  • The plan targets net-zero emissions by mid-century.
  • It ties climate action to affordability and public health.

Geneva Avenue Upgrades Accepted for Housing Site

  • The mayor introduced a bill to accept new Geneva Avenue infrastructure.
  • The work is tied to the affordable housing project at 2340 San Jose Avenue.
  • It sets official sidewalk widths and waives certain Admin Code rules.
  • Property transfers to Public Works under the bill.
